Stump Grinding Service for Lehi & Utah County

After a tree is removed, the stump left behind is more than an eyesore — it's a trip hazard, a pest magnet, and an obstacle to your landscaping plans. Our stump grinding service eliminates stumps quickly, completely, and affordably.

We use professional-grade stump grinders to grind stumps 6–12 inches below ground level, leaving behind only wood chips that can be used as mulch or hauled away. Within weeks, the area is ready for grass seed, sod, or new planting.

Reasons to Grind Your Stump

  • Safety — stumps are a tripping hazard for kids, guests, and elderly family members
  • Pests — decaying stumps attract termites, carpenter ants, beetles, and other wood-boring insects
  • Disease — diseased stumps can spread root rot to healthy nearby trees
  • Regrowth — some species (like cottonwood and elm) will sprout multiple shoots from an unground stump
  • Mowing — stumps make mowing difficult and can damage lawn equipment
  • Aesthetics — a bare stump detracts from your yard's appearance and home value
  • Landscaping — grinding opens space for new planting, sod, patios, or construction

Our Stump Grinding Process

  • We call 811 before grinding near utility lines (always included)
  • Grind the stump 6–12" below grade depending on your plans for the space
  • Surface roots are ground back as needed
  • Wood chips are raked and spread as mulch or loaded for removal
  • We fill the void with chips or topsoil — your choice
  • The area is left clean and ready for your next step

Frequently Asked Questions About Stump Grinding

Do I really need to remove the stump?

Not always, but many homeowners choose stump grinding because stumps can attract insects, interfere with mowing, and create a tripping hazard.

How deep do you grind the stump?

Most stumps are ground several inches below grade, allowing the area to be covered with soil, mulch, or grass afterward.

Will the roots keep growing after stump grinding?

In most cases, the root system gradually dies back once the stump is removed. Some species may produce occasional shoots, but this is usually manageable.

Can I plant grass where the stump used to be?

Yes. After grinding and cleanup, many homeowners reseed the area or install sod to restore a seamless lawn.

How long does stump grinding take?

Most residential stumps can be completed in under an hour, though larger stumps may take longer.
